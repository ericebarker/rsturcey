What is Embodied Imagery Psychotherapy?


Embodied Imagery (EI) is a non-interpretive way of entering into a deeper, mind/ body integrated experience of our dreams, memories and life experiences that have been slowed down, and distilled into high energy moments, points of contact for deep diving into Depth. We allow the sense of meaningfulness to unfold, rather than seeking a "meaning" that may then close one off to the sense of mystery held within the imagery or moment. EI creates an alternative to remaining within our fixed, "habitual consciousness," and usual way of thinking. We remain open to the Mystery of dreams, as well as becoming open to deeper entry into our outer life experience, slowing down the poignant moments, moving from the ones laden with emotion, to those barren of feeling, with many variations along the way, always slowly, frame by frame. We simply are deeply present to what IS. By doing so, we lean into the unique quality, emotion and physically felt location within the body, and what is contained within the depths of the image/experience. The Sacred Container of the relationship between individual and psychotherapist becomes the Space to hold the creative work to unfold, for change and transformation to unfold from our depths, rather than from attempting to force change from the habitual consciousness perspective. For a deeper explanation of Embodied Imagery, please refer to the Writings tab.


Bio


Ruby began her career by becoming certified as a Professionally trained midwife in Derby, England in 1980. She attended births in Texas, Massachusettes and New York. In 1989, following the birth of her third child, she retired for good from practicing midwifery. Ruby became a registered nurse, with a career including work as a Hospice nurse, again working with many individuals and their families from a wide variety of ethnic, religious and socio-economic backgrounds. She continues to work as a psychiatric nurse with individuals suffering largely from PTSD as well as other psychiatric issues. She received a Bachelor's degree majoring in Jungian studies and Holistic health, and a Master of Arts degree from Lesley University in Cambridge Massachusettes. Ruby has had a life long passionate interest in the imagination and dreams, and has been consistently recording her own dreams since her late teens. After studying a variety of methods of engaging with dreams, she discovered the work of Robert Bosnak, Jungian analyst and author. She began training with Bosnak in 2004, after reading his book "A Little Course in Dreams" and then having the good fortune to begin attending the Manhatten Seminars in NYC, NY. She completed a three year Embodied Imagery Psychotherapy program and was certified in 2008. She currently has a private practice in Embodied Imagery Psychotherapy.
